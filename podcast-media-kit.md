## üí¨ ANTICIPATED QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

**Q: "How can a $75 Pi compete with NVIDIA's $3,000 machine?"**
A: Different models, different trade-offs. NVIDIA's box runs larger models faster. Our Pi runs smaller, efficient models (like Phi-3) that handle 90% of real-world use cases. Think "good enough" at 1/40th the cost.

**Q: "Isn't this just for hobbyists?"**
A: Tell that to the 50,000 Nigerian patients being diagnosed with AI, or the Japanese startup that saved $150K/year and reached profitability. This is production-grade, just optimized differently.

**Q: "What about performance?"**
A: Phi-3 Mini on Pi: ~2 seconds per response. Cloud APIs: 3-5 seconds. We're often faster because there's no network latency. Plus: 100% uptime (no API outages).

**Q: "Why should I care about 'digital sovereignty'?"**
A: Ask what happens when your cloud AI provider raises prices 300% (happened with multiple services). Or cuts off your region. Or goes out of business. Owning your infrastructure means owning your future.

**Q: "What's the catch?"**
A: Smaller models. You won't run GPT-4-equivalent on a Pi. But Phi-3 (3.8B parameters) handles chat, code completion, summarization, classification - most real-world tasks.

**Q: "How technical do I need to be?"**
A: If you can copy-paste into terminal, you can do this. Our installer script does everything. 30 minutes from unboxing to running AI.

**Q: "What about power consumption?"**
A: Pi draws 15W max. That's less than a light bulb. NVIDIA's box: 500W. Over a year, Pi costs ~$20 in electricity. NVIDIA: ~$650.

**Q: "Is this legal?"**
A: 100%. We use open-source models (Phi-3 is MIT licensed), open-source software (Ollama), and hardware you own. No TOS violations, no API limits.

**Q: "What's next for the project?"**
A: We're at 2,847 nodes. Goal: 10,000 by end of year. Building mesh networking for distributed inference. Creating more success stories. Proving edge AI is the future.
